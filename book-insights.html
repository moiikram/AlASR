Riyad as-Salihin

Hadith 194: Abu Sa'id Al-Khudri (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The best type of Jihad (striving in the way of Allah) is speaking a true word in the presence of a tyrant ruler."

أفضل الجهاد كلمة عدل عند سلطان جائر

[Abu Dawud and At-Tirmidhi, categorized as Hadith Hasan]

Hadith 195: Abu Sa'id Al-Khudri (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: A man came to the Prophet (ﷺ) and said, "Advise me." He (ﷺ) said, "Fear Allah for it is the sum of all good, and engage in Jihad for it is the monasticism of a Muslim, and remember Allah much for it will be your protection from the punishment of Allah."

اتق الله فإنه جماع الخير، وجاهد فإنه رهبانية المسلم، واذكر الله كثيرًا فإنه وقايتك من عذاب الله

[At-Tabarani]

Hadith 196: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "He who dies without having fought in the Cause of Allah or without having the intention to do so, dies on a branch of hypocrisy."

من مات ولم يغز ولم يحدث نفسه بالغزو مات على شعبة من النفاق

[Muslim]

Hadith 197: Tariq bin Shihab (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: A Jew said to 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with him), "If this verse 'This day I have perfected for you your religion...' [Quran 5:3] had been revealed to us, we would have taken that day as a festival." 'Umar said, "I know the day and place where it was revealed; it was revealed to the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) on the Day of 'Arafah while he was standing there."

قال يهودي لعمر: لو نزلت علينا هذه الآية "اليوم أكملت لكم دينكم" لاتخذنا ذلك اليوم عيدًا، فقال عمر: إني لأعلم اليوم والمكان الذي نزلت فيه، نزلت على رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يوم عرفة وهو واقف بعرفة

[Al-Bukhari and Muslim]

Hadith 198: 'Aishah (May Allah be pleased with her) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "He who loves to meet Allah, Allah loves to meet him; and he who hates to meet Allah, Allah hates to meet him."

من أحب لقاء الله أحب الله لقاءه، ومن كره لقاء الله كره الله لقاءه

[Al-Bukhari and Muslim]

Hadith 199: Abu Musa (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Whoever loves his worldly life will harm his Hereafter, and whoever loves his Hereafter will harm his worldly life. So prefer that which is everlasting over that which is temporary."

من أحب دنياه أضر بآخرته، ومن أحب آخرته أضر بدنياه، فآثروا ما يبقى على ما يفنى

[Ad-Daraqutni and Al-Baihaqi]

Hadith 200: Sahl bin Sa'd (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "If the world was worth the wing of a mosquito in the Sight of Allah, He would not have given a disbeliever a sip of water from it."

لو كانت الدنيا تعدل عند الله جناح بعوضة ما سقى كافرًا منها شربة ماء

[At-Tirmidhi, categorized as Hadith Hasan Sahih]

Hadith 201: Hudhaifah and Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with them) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Allah will assemble mankind, and the believers will stand till Jannah is brought near them. They will then go to Adam (ﷺ) and say, 'O our father, ask Allah that Jannah may be opened for us,' but he will reply: 'There was nothing that put you out of Jannah except your father's sin.'"

يجمع الله الناس فيقوم المؤمنون حتى يدنى لهم الجنة، فيأتون آدم عليه السلام فيقولون: يا أبانا استفتح لنا الجنة، فيقول: وهل أخرجكم من الجنة إلا خطيئة أبيكم

[Muslim]
